# Domain Modeling Summary
## Carton Caps Conversational Assistant

---

## What Did We Build in This Phase?

In the previous phase we understood the problem: what Carton Caps is, who uses it, and what the system needs to accomplish. In Phase 2, we turned that understanding into a **precise blueprint** — a shared language and a set of rules that define exactly how the system must behave, before a single line of code is written.

This blueprint is called the **Domain Model**. It captures the real-world concepts the system works with (users, sessions, products, schools), the processes that have lifecycles and states (conversations, decision profiles), and the rules that must never be broken under any circumstances.

---

## Why Does the Domain Model Matter?

Think of the Domain Model as the **constitution** of the system.

Before deciding which AI framework to use, which database to connect, or how the API looks — the constitution defines what is always true. It says: a conversation session cannot become active before it is initialized. A recommendation can only cite products that were actually retrieved. The user's school is always known before the first message is processed. These are not preferences or best practices. They are invariants — rules the system cannot violate regardless of what surrounds it.

This matters because the system handles real fundraising activity for real schools. If the assistant invents a product recommendation, gives incorrect referral guidance, or loses track of what a user said two turns ago, it breaks the trust of parents and undermines the fundraising mission. The Domain Model exists to make those failures structurally impossible — not just unlikely.

# Part 1 — For Non-Technical Readers

## What is this system?

Carton Caps is a school fundraising platform. Parents buy everyday products — snacks, household items, food — and a portion of each purchase goes to their child's school. The more parents buy, and the more they invite other parents to join, the more money the school receives.

The challenge is that parents often don't know what to buy, or don't know how to invite others, or simply forget the platform exists. The assistant is designed to solve this.

---

## What does the assistant do?

Think of it as a **personal shopping advisor** that also cares about your school.

When you open a conversation with the assistant, it already knows:
- Who you are
- Which school you support
- What you've talked about before (if you've used it previously)

From there, you have a natural conversation. You might say:
> *"My kids are picky eaters and I want to buy something healthy."*

The assistant listens, remembers that detail, and starts building a picture of what you need. As the conversation continues, it gets smarter — and eventually, it recommends specific products from the catalog that match your situation. Every recommendation is real — no invented products, no guesses.

At the same time, the assistant looks for moments to remind you how the referral program works. If you could earn extra donations for your school by inviting a friend, it will tell you exactly how.

---

## How does a conversation actually work?

Here is what happens from the moment you send a message:

**1. You send a message.**
The system identifies whether you're asking about a product, asking about referrals, answering a question the assistant asked, or just chatting.

**2. Your preferences are noted.**
Any useful signal — "picky eaters," "prefer healthy," "tight budget" — is extracted and saved as part of your decision profile for this conversation.

**3. The assistant checks what it knows about you so far.**
If it has enough information to make a good recommendation, it proceeds. If it still needs more, it asks one focused question — not a survey, just one question.

**4. A search is performed.**
Not a keyword search — a smart, context-aware search. The assistant builds a search query based on everything it now knows about you, then looks up the product catalog or referral rules.

**5. The assistant answers.**
The response is built entirely from what was found. It cites real products, real rules. And every product suggestion includes a reminder: *"and every purchase supports [your school's name]."*

**6. The conversation continues.**
Your profile keeps growing. Each message makes the next recommendation more precise.

---

## What the assistant is NOT

It is not a search box. Typing "snacks" does not return a list.

It is not a chatbot that answers generic questions. It actively guides you toward a decision.

It is not a recommendation engine that ignores context. It only recommends after it understands your situation.

It is a **fundraising co-pilot** — a system that turns conversation into action that supports your school.

# Part 2 — Technical Summary

## System Purpose

> Transform multi-turn conversation into structured decision context that drives context-enriched retrieval, grounded product recommendations, and referral guidance — all in service of increasing school donations.

The system is designed as a **stateful conversational RAG service**. It is not a Q&A system. Every design decision is oriented toward one outcome: guiding the user toward a purchase or referral action that benefits their school.

---

## Functional Requirements — What the System Must Do

Eight functional requirements define the system's behavior:

| ID | Requirement | Core Behavior |
|---|---|---|
| FR1 | Session Management | Maintain stateful multi-turn sessions; pre-seed context with school and history at start |
| FR2 | Intent Recognition | Classify every message before processing: `product_inquiry`, `referral_question`, `general_question`, `clarification_response`, `ambiguous` |
| FR3 | Decision Context Construction | Extract `PreferenceSignal` values each turn; accumulate into `DecisionContext` |
| FR4 | Context-Enriched Knowledge Retrieval | Build retrieval query from `intent + context` (never raw message); route to correct source; evaluate context quality (recall + precision); inject evidence; evaluate response quality (groundedness + relevance) |
| FR5 | Product Recommendation | Recommend only retrieved products; always connect recommendation to school fundraising goal |
| FR6 | Referral Guidance | Explain referral program using retrieved rules only; never from LLM memory |
| FR7 | Clarifying Question Generation | Ask one targeted question when context gaps prevent a good recommendation |
| FR8 | Conversational API Endpoint | Expose REST API: `POST /sessions` (open session), `POST /sessions/{id}/turns` (process message), `DELETE /sessions/{id}` (close session); return grounded response with quality scores |

**Key architectural assumption:** User authentication is handled by an existing external REST API. Every request arrives with a pre-resolved, trusted `user_id`. This service never authenticates.

---

## System Workflows — How the System Behaves

Four workflows define the behavioral flow:

**WF0 — Conversational Response Loop** *(primary, runs every turn)*
```
receive message → classify intent → update context (WF1)
  → construct retrieval query → retrieve evidence (WF2 or WF3)
  → evaluate context quality [recall + precision]
  → generate grounded response
  → evaluate response quality [groundedness + relevance]
  → return validated response
```

**WF1 — Decision Context Building** *(secondary, mandatory every turn)*
Extracts preference signals from the current message. Updates `DecisionContext`. Evaluates gaps. Emits clarification request if needed. Pre-seeded at session start with school, prior conversation history, and purchase history (if available).

**WF2 — Product Recommendation** *(secondary, activates on `product_inquiry` intent or when context is `ready`)*
Constructs enriched retrieval query from context. Queries product catalog. Evaluates context quality (recall + precision) before injection. Injects retrieved products into generation. Recommendation explicitly tied to school fundraising goal.

**WF3 — Referral Guidance** *(secondary, activates on `referral_question` intent)*
Constructs retrieval query from fundraising context signals. Queries referral rules knowledge base. Evaluates context quality before injection. Builds guidance strictly from retrieved rules. Suggests a specific referral action.

**Routing rule:** WF1 always activates. WF2 and WF3 activate based on intent and context state. Both can activate in the same turn.

## Domain Concepts — The Building Blocks

### Process Entities (stateful domain objects with lifecycle)

**`ConversationSession`**
The top-level container. One session per user interaction. Everything happens inside it.
Lifecycle: `created → initializing → idle ⇄ active → closed`
- `initializing`: loads user, school, conversation history, purchase history from DB; pre-seeds `DecisionContext`
- `active`: processing a turn
- `idle`: waiting for next message
- `closed`: terminal — no recovery

**`DecisionContext`**
The intelligence asset of the system. Accumulates preference signals across turns. Drives retrieval query construction. Never regresses once it reaches `ready`.
Lifecycle: `empty → partial → ready → enriched`
- `empty`: only school anchor present (pre-seeded)
- `partial`: signals accumulating, gaps still exist
- `ready`: sufficient signals for a meaningful retrieval query and recommendation
- `enriched`: deep context, highly precise retrieval

### Entities

| Entity | Role |
|---|---|
| `User` | Pre-resolved identity; school association loaded from DB |
| `School` | Fundraising anchor; always present in `DecisionContext` from session start |
| `Product` | Catalog item; retrieved never invented; carries fundraising association |
| `RetrievalQuery` | Constructed from `intent + context`; routes to the right knowledge source |
| `ConversationHistory` | Persisted prior turns; loaded at session start to restore context |
| `PurchaseHistory` | Optional behavioral signal; currently empty; graceful-degradation design |

### Value Objects

| Value Object | Role |
|---|---|
| `Intent` | Immutable per-turn classification (`product_inquiry`, `referral_question`, `general_question`, `clarification_response`, `ambiguous`) |
| `PreferenceSignal` | Single extracted preference; immutable; accumulates monotonically |
| `RetrievedEvidence` | Knowledge returned by retrieval; carries `context_recall_score` and `context_precision_score` evaluated before injection |
| `AssistantResponse` | Grounded response; carries `groundedness_score` and `relevance_score`; returned only if both meet threshold |
| `ReferralRule` | Platform rule; retrieved; never invented by LLM |

## Invariants — The Rules That Must Never Break

30 invariants across 6 groups enforce domain correctness at every point:

**Session invariants (INV-CS-*):**
- Session states are strictly ordered; `closed` is terminal
- Initialization cannot be skipped; active state requires completed initialization
- Exactly one `DecisionContext` per active session
- No concurrent sessions for the same user
- Every request carries a pre-resolved `user_id`; this service never authenticates

**Context invariants (INV-DC-*):**
- `school` is always present — never null, never changes during the session
- Context never regresses: `ready → partial` is illegal; `enriched → ready` is illegal
- Preference signals accumulate monotonically — never removed
- `ready` state requires at least one `PreferenceSignal` beyond the pre-seeded school

**Retrieval query invariants (INV-RQ-*):**
- Query always constructed from `intent + context`, never from the raw user message alone
- `source_target` must match routing: `product_inquiry → product_catalog`, `referral_question → referral_program_rules`

**Response invariants (INV-AR-*):**
- Every response grounded in evidence; `groundedness_score` must meet threshold (≥ 0.8) before response is returned
- LLM cannot invent products; all product recommendations traceable to retrieved evidence
- Referral guidance derives only from retrieved rules — not LLM memory
- Every product recommendation references the user's school fundraising goal
- If `groundedness_score` or `relevance_score` falls below threshold, response is blocked; safe fallback returned
- A grounded-but-irrelevant response (answers a different question) is still a failure

**Retrieval evidence invariants (INV-RE-*):**
- `context_recall_score` and `context_precision_score` computed after every retrieval call, before injection
- If recall < threshold, query is refined and retrieval retried — degraded evidence must not proceed to generation

**Workflow ordering invariants (INV-WF-*):**
- Strict turn ordering: intent classification → context update → retrieval query construction → retrieval → **context quality evaluation** → generation → **response quality evaluation** → return
- WF1 cannot be skipped on any turn

## Bounded Contexts — The Semantic Boundaries

Three bounded contexts define the system's architecture. The context map mirrors the RAG pipeline exactly.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│   Conversation Management       │
│  ConversationSession            │
│  ConversationHistory            │
│  User · School (reference)      │
│  Intent · AssistantResponse     │
└────────────┬────────────────────┘
             │ classified Intent + message
             ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│   Decision Intelligence         │
│  DecisionContext                │
│  RetrievalQuery                 │
│  PreferenceSignal               │
└────────────┬────────────────────┘
             │ RetrievalQuery (context-enriched)
             ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│   Knowledge Retrieval           │
│  Product · ReferralRule         │
│  RetrievedEvidence              │
└────────────┬────────────────────┘
             │ RetrievedEvidence
             ▼
   Decision Intelligence → Conversation Management
   (evidence injected → grounded response assembled)
```

**BC-1 — Conversation Management**
Owns session lifecycle, turn orchestration, intent classification, and response delivery. Loads all DB data at session start. Enforces turn ordering.

**BC-2 — Decision Intelligence**
Owns the `DecisionContext` lifecycle and all retrieval query construction. The core intelligence of the system. Enforces the no-regression invariant. Builds retrieval queries always from `intent + context`.

**BC-3 — Knowledge Retrieval**
Owns the product catalog and referral program rules as searchable knowledge sources. Executes semantic retrieval. Returns `RetrievedEvidence`. Never invents content.

**Why this decomposition works:**
- Each context has a distinct semantic language and non-overlapping invariants
- The context interaction map is exactly the RAG pipeline — no extra translation needed
- `RetrievedEvidence` flows cleanly across contexts without ownership conflict
- `School` is pre-seeded by BC-1 into BC-2 at session start — no cross-context lookup required during turns

## Key Design Insights

**1. The retrieval query is never the raw user message.**
This is the system's defining design decision. The query is always `intent + accumulated DecisionContext`. This is what makes retrieval progressively more precise as the conversation grows.

**2. Context accumulation is monotonic and irreversible.**
Once a `PreferenceSignal` is added, it stays. Once `DecisionContext` reaches `ready`, it never regresses. The system builds trust in its own reasoning over time.

**3. All responses are grounded in retrieved evidence — and evaluated before delivery.**
The LLM cannot invent products or referral rules. Every recommendation is traceable to a specific retrieval result. `groundedness_score` is computed after generation and the response is blocked if it falls below threshold. `relevance_score` ensures the response addresses the correct intent — not a related but different question.

**4. The school is always present.**
Pre-seeded before the first turn. Present in every recommendation. Not something the assistant needs to ask — it already knows. This anchors every conversation in the fundraising mission.

**5. The bounded context map and the RAG pipeline are structurally identical.**
BC-1 → BC-2 → BC-3 → BC-2 → BC-1. This is not coincidental — it means the architecture will directly express the domain logic without translation layers.

**6. Purchase history is empty today, designed for tomorrow.**
The system gracefully degrades when purchase history is not available. When it becomes available, it automatically enriches context without requiring redesign.

**7. RAG evaluation is built into the pipeline, not bolted on.**
Three evaluation gates enforce the invariants at runtime: context quality check (recall + precision on `RetrievedEvidence` before injection), groundedness check (claim traceability on `AssistantResponse` after generation), and relevance check (intent alignment via reverse-question technique). These gates are the runtime enforcement of INV-AR-1 through INV-AR-6 and INV-RE-1 through INV-RE-4. Without them, the invariants are design aspirations. With them, they are enforced constraints.
